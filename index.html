ESG

Investment for Better World



ESG investing involves investments on stocks of firms with high rankings on their sustainability practices which make positive environmental, social, and corporate governance impacts. The score rubrics for these rankings normally are based on the UN Sustainable Development Goals which acknowledge that significant private sector action is needed to foster the goals. The US SIF provides some examples of corporate activities causing ESG impacts.

ESG

Rankings & Net Benefits to Firms and Investors



Scores for ESG rankings often are gathered from companies' sustainability reports. Governance and Accountability Institute's research show results of large increase in S&P 500 companies sustainability reports from 20% in 2011 to 86% in 2018 reflecting benefits to firms to voluntarily self-report their ESG activities.

Firms increase risk assessments as practices to generate data and content for reports, resulting in efficient operations and higher net benefits. Investors remains skeptical since standards for ESG rankings remain unestablished. Some firms now hire independent ESG quality assurance companies to improve accuracy of their reports. An academic publication from Harvard Business School also provide(s) evidence that High Sustainability companies significantly outperform their counterparts over the long-term, both in terms of stock market as well as accounting performance.

Firm Profiles and S&P ESG 500 Index Weight



Top ESG firms using the SNPE ETF criteria

SNPE is an ESG focused fund that draws its universe from the S&P 500. The fund excludes companies involved in tobacco, controversial weapons, ranks low with the United Nations Global Compact (“UNGC”) score ranking, and score bottom 25% of GICS ESG ratings within its industry. After exclusions are applied, companies are selected by ESG score, while at the same time mirroring the industry weightings of the S&P 500.

Disclaimer: This webpage neither relates nore endorses SNPE. They are used for educational purposes only as their criteria are closely related to the UN Sustainable Development Goals.
Note: Sustainable development is defined as “development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.”
